Click here for my resume.

I’m a computational social scientist and a member of the CSS London initiative. Currently, I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Methodology Department of the London School of Economics as a part of the Quanteda Initiative.

I am also a research scientist working on an automated newspaper reporting extraction project using natural language processing (NLP) at Uppsala University (Sweden), in conjunction with Research Institutes of Sweden (RISE).

My ongoing research primarily focuses on predictive models of armed conflict dynamics; exploring the tensions between explanatory vs. predictive research; textual analysis of political violence event data; and the circulation and dissemination of social science research.

I take inspiration from physics, biology, and complex systems theory, which allow me to employ various mainstream statistical and machine learning tools to solve various social science problems at hand. Most of my work is on GitHub. I happily contribute my posts to R-Bloggers, a hub for all things R that I have learned a lot from, and RWeekly. Check out R-Users as well, a job posting platform for R enthusiasts.
