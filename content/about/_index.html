Click here for my resume.

I’m a computational social scientist and a member of the CSS London initiative. Currently, I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Methodology Department of the London School of Economics. As a member of the Quanteda Initiative, I am responsible for developing a GUI for the quanteda package in R.

In addition, I am a research scientist at Uppsala University (in conjunction with the Research Institutes of Sweden–RISE) on an automated newspaper report extraction project using natural language processing.

My ongoing research primarily focuses on predictive models of armed conflict dynamics, structural tensions between explanatory versus predictive research, textual analysis of political violence event data; as well as the issues of representation and bias in social science research.

I take inspiration from physics, biology, and complex systems theory, which allow me to employ various mainstream statistical and advanced machine learning tools to solve various social science problems at the intersection of conflict studies, political science, and international relations.

Most of my work is on GitHub. I happily contribute my posts to R-Bloggers, a hub for all things R that I have learned a lot from, and RWeekly. Check out R-Users as well, a job posting platform for R enthusiasts.
