Click here for my resume.

I’m a computational social scientist specialising in conflict forecasting.

My core research focuses on predictive models of armed conflict dynamics; problematising the tensions between explanatory versus predictive research; textual analysis of political violence event-data; and the quantification of issues of representation and bias in social science research.

My methodological work revolves around making black box machine learning findings more human-friendly, introducing new procedures for cross-validating models using binary-time-series-cross-section data, and developing a random forest algorithm that can deal with time-varying covariates.

Currently, I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Department of Methodology at the London School of Economics. As a member of the Quanteda Initiative, I am responsible for developing a GUI for the quanteda package in R. I am also a member of the CSS London initiative. In addition, I am a research scientist at Uppsala University working on (in conjunction with the Research Institutes of Sweden–RISE) the automation of the Uppsala Conflict Data Program (UCDP) using natural language processing (NLP).

I take inspiration from statistical mechanics and complex systems theory, which allow me to employ various mainstream statistical and advanced machine learning tools to solve various social science problems at the intersection of conflict studies, political science, and international relations.

Most of my work is on GitHub. I am happy to contribute my blog to R-Bloggers, a hub for all things R that I have learned a lot from, and RWeekly.
