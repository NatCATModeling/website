Works in Progress


Gokhan Ciflikli and Nils Metternich. Improving civil war duration prediction with ensemble learning. This paper introduces statistical learning techniques to the study of conflict duration and presents a framework to predict ethnic conflict durations from actor and group configurations. Using EPR data to identify which ethnic groups are part of the government and rebel organizations, we build an ensemble model for ethnic conflict duration prediction.


Nils Metternich, Gokhan Ciflikli, Ali Altaf, and Tatjana Stankovic. Predicting the severity of civil Wars: An actor-centric approach. This paper presents an actor-centric approach to predict the severity of armed conflict. We argue that the prediction of conflict severity needs to focus on the actors that are responsible for conducting armed violence. Our statistical learning approach relies on random forests to predict the severity of conflict. Further, we introduce two open source packages for conflict scholars: rebelTrack, a utility tool for UCDP GED Event dataset; and rebelCast, an intutitive random forest fitter for time-series-cross-sectional data.
